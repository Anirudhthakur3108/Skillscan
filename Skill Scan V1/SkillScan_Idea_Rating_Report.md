# SkillScan Idea Rating Report (Brainstorming Review)

Date: 2026-03-22
Scope reviewed: `Proposal.docx`, `SkillScan Business Report.docx`, `Market Gap Analysis & Business Solution Development for SkillScan (College Student Edition).docx`

## 1) Understanding Summary
- SkillScan addresses a real and large pain: students overstate resume skills while employers and placement cells cannot verify practical competency quickly.
- The primary starting segment is Tier-2/3 college students, with a niche-first rollout (Business Analytics MBA -> ERP -> Data Science BCA).
- Core value proposition is "resume-claimed skill verification + quantified level score + 30-day action roadmap" rather than generic aptitude testing.
- The product serves two stakeholders: students (self-improvement and credibility) and colleges (batch-level skill-gap dashboard and placement planning).
- Differentiation is local, role-linked verification and college integration, not broad nationwide exam-style assessment.
- MVP feasibility is strong because initial stack and workflow are simple enough for a small pilot build.
- Long-term success depends less on AI extraction quality and more on assessment validity, badge trust, and institutional adoption.

## 2) Assumptions (Explicit)
- The cited employability and mismatch figures are directionally correct and credible enough for decision-making.
- Students are willing to pay around INR 99 for premium assessment output if perceived value is immediate.
- Placement cells can allocate budget/time for dashboards if reports clearly improve placement metrics.
- A 1-10 skill score can be made defensible through question quality and anti-cheating controls.
- Recruiters/colleges will treat "SkillScan Verified" as useful only if assessment rigor is visible and consistent.
- Initial pilot can recruit a minimum of 100-300 students through existing college networks.

## 3) Open Questions
- What is the exact benchmark definition for each score level (e.g., SQL L5 vs L7)?
- How will test integrity be protected (copying, AI-assisted answering, answer sharing)?
- What evidence package will make recruiters trust the badge beyond a visual label?
- Which stakeholder is primary in Year 1 revenue: students or colleges?
- What is the minimum data needed to prove placement impact within one academic cycle?

## 5) Strengths
- Strong problem-solution fit in an underserved Tier-2/3 context.
- Practical niche-first sequencing instead of "build for everyone".
- Dual-sided value proposition (student + college) with clear outcomes.
- Feasible MVP path and low initial capital requirement.

## 6) Key Risks
- Assessment validity risk: weak questions will collapse trust quickly.
- Badge credibility risk: without external recognition, verification may be seen as cosmetic.
- Adoption risk: placement cells may delay purchase until measurable outcomes are proven.
- Integrity risk: cheating or leaked questions can distort score reliability.
- Outcome attribution risk: proving placement improvement may require longer observation windows.

## 7) Brainstormed Improvement Directions (Before Scale)
1. Make scoring transparent: publish skill rubrics and sample level descriptors so L1-L10 is explainable.
2. Add trust signals: timed sections, question pools, retest logic, and a proctoring-lite integrity layer.
3. Launch proof pilots: run 2-3 controlled college pilots and compare pre/post skill distributions.
4. Sharpen economics: track CAC, conversion to paid, repeat usage, and college renewal intent from day one.
5. Strengthen badge utility: include verifiable score report links and timestamped assessment metadata.

## 8) Decision Log (Brainstorming)
- Decision: Keep Tier-2/3 niche-first strategy as the core launch thesis.
- Alternatives considered: Broad all-college launch from day one.
- Reason chosen: Niche-first reduces complexity, improves message relevance, and lowers early GTM cost.

- Decision: Prioritize assessment rigor and trust mechanics ahead of feature expansion.
- Alternatives considered: Add more skills/features first for growth optics.
- Reason chosen: In verification products, trust is the product; weak rigor undermines all growth.

- Decision: Treat Year-1 projections as hypotheses, not commitments.
- Alternatives considered: Commit to aggressive forecast as baseline plan.
- Reason chosen: Early-stage education products require pilot evidence before reliable scaling assumptions.

## 9) Final Verdict
SkillScan is a **high-potential idea with credible market pain and practical MVP feasibility**. It is currently strongest as a **pilot-stage venture thesis** and should be advanced with disciplined validation focused on **assessment quality, trust, and measurable placement-related outcomes**.

Readiness classification: **Proceed to pilot (not full scale yet).**
